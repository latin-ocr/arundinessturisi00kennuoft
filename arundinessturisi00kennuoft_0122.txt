XXV.

UXELLA.

In tumulum veteris Uxellae ruinas despicientem.

Quis fuit, antiqu& Corini de gente, sepulchrum
Cui patria hic, tumido in cespite, grata dedit?

Quo fato occubuit? Romae victricibus armis,
An debellati Saxonis ense cadens?

Uxellae periere arces, disjectaque circum
Strata jacent foedo regia tecta situ:

At durat lapis aggestus bis mille per annos,

Et vano ignotum signat honore ducem.

IDEM ANGLICE.

Inscription sor a stone to be placed on a barrow near
Lcstwithiel.

Of Cornwall's ancient race what chieftain brave
Found in this sweliing turf an honour'd grave?

What fate subdued thee? Rome's all conquering band,

Or some defeated Saxon's vengeful hand?

Lo! time bath whelm’d Lestwithiel's regal haus,
And scarce Restormel shows her ivied walls:

Yet still thro’ many an age these gather'd stones
With vain memorial grace thy long forgotten bones.

Idem.
