POEMA TA VARJA. 109

LATINE REDDITU M.

Ah, formosa levi nimium si cedat Amori
Femina, et inveniat prodere posse viros ς
Carmine quo posset saevum lenire dolorem?
Quove piare scelus sola relicta modo?

Omnibus illaesum si vult servare pudorem,
Perjuri pectus tangere posse viri,
Atque animum vero demum torquere dolore,
Certa salus miserae panditur una—mori.
R. B. K.

XXVIII.

GALLIA FU RENS.

(Verses written at Walmer Castle‚ by desire os Mr. Pitt, in the month
of September, 1797. A copy of these verses was left with
Mr. Pitt‚ when Lord NVellesley embarked sor India on the
7th November, 1797. They were published in the Anti-
Jacobin‚ No. VI, December 18th‚ 1797.)

Ipsa mali Hortatrix scelerumque uberrima Mater
In se prima suos vertit lymphata furores,
Luctaturque diu secum, et conatibus aegris

Fessa cadit, proprioque jacet labefacta veneno.
Mox tamen ipsius rursum violentia morbi

Erigit ardentem furiis‚,ultroque minantem
Spargere bella procul, vastaeque incendia cladis,
Civilesque agitare faces, totumque per orbem
Sceptra super Regum, et Populorum subdita colla
Ferre pedem, et sanctas Regnorum evertere sedes.
